Brazilian Flavors

Picanha Steak:

Discover the savory delight of grilled Picanha, a uniquely Brazilian cut of steak from the rump cap muscle, offering a tender and flavorful dining experience. Also known as culotte, top sirloin cap, or rump cover, this triangular cut boasts versatility and robust flavor. Begin by salting the picanha up to 24 hours in advance, allowing the flavors to penetrate and enhance the meat's texture. When ready to grill, employ the reverse sear method by slowly cooking the picanha over low heat until it reaches an internal temperature of 115°F, ensuring a juicy and tender outcome. Then, sear the steak over medium-high heat for a brief period on each side until it reaches 130°F, achieving a perfect medium-rare finish. While resting the meat is optional, it allows the flavors to meld and intensify, further enhancing your dining experience. With its succulent texture and mouthwatering flavor, grilled Picanha is sure to elevate any barbecue or dinner gathering to new heights of culinary excellence.

Grilled Pineapple:

Prepare for a taste bud explosion with the irresistible flavors of Brazilian grilled pineapple. Begin by preheating your outdoor grill to medium-high heat and lightly oiling the grate for easy cooking. In a bowl, combine brown sugar and ground cinnamon, creating a sweet and aromatic coating for the pineapple wedges. Transfer the sugar mixture to a large resealable plastic bag, add the pineapple wedges, and shake vigorously to ensure each wedge is thoroughly coated. Grill the pineapple wedges on the preheated grill until heated through and caramelized, approximately 3 to 5 minutes per side, infusing them with a delightful smoky flavor and enhancing their natural sweetness. With each juicy bite, savor the perfect balance of sweet and tangy notes, making this Brazilian grilled pineapple a mouthwatering addition to any barbecue or summer gathering.

Dulce De Leche:

Indulge in the creamy decadence of homemade dulce de leche with this irresistible recipe. Begin by preheating the oven to 400°F and pouring sweetened condensed milk into a baking dish, then cover it with aluminum foil and place it in a larger dish filled with hot water. Let it bake for 90 minutes until it transforms into velvety dulce de leche. Meanwhile, prepare a delightful graham cracker crust by blending graham crackers with melted butter and sugar, then bake until golden and fragrant. Once cooled, assemble the mini snack cups by layering the graham cracker crust, luscious dulce de leche, and sliced bananas, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours to set. Before serving, top each cup with a dollop of freshly whipped cream, adding a touch of airy sweetness to this indulgent treat. With each creamy spoonful, savor the rich caramel flavor and smooth texture of this homemade dulce de leche dessert, sure to delight your taste buds and leave you craving more.
